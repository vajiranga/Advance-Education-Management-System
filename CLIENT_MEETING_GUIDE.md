## üó£Ô∏è 2. The Presentation Strategy (‡∂â‡∂Ø‡∑í‡∂ª‡∑í‡∂¥‡∂≠‡∑ä ‡∂ö‡∂ª‡∂± ‡∂¥‡∑í‡∑Ö‡∑í‡∑Ä‡∑ô‡∂Ω)

Clients care about **Visuals** and **Money**, not Code.

1.  **Start with the "Wow" Factor (Visuals):**
    - Open the **Admin Dashboard** first.
    - Show the new colorful cards (Revenue, Students) and charts. This gives a confident first impression.
2.  **Show the Money Flow (Business Value):**
    - Show how a **Student pays fees** (uploading a slip or manual payment).
    - Show how the **Admin approves it**.
    - **Crucial:** Show the "Teacher Settlements" report. Clients love seeing how the system handles money for them.
3.  **Parent/Student Experience:**
    - Briefly show the Student portal to prove it works.

## üß† 3. Handling Questions & New Requests (‡∂ú‡∑ê‡∂ß‡∑Ö‡∑î ‡∑É‡∑Ñ ‡∂Ö‡∂Ω‡∑î‡∂≠‡∑ä ‡∂â‡∂Ω‡∑ä‡∂Ω‡∑ì‡∂∏‡∑ä)

This is where most beginners get stuck.

- **Scenario A: The Client finds a bug.**
  - _Don't Panic._
  - _Don't say:_ "Oh no, I don't know why that happened."
  - _Do say:_ "That‚Äôs interesting. I‚Äôll make a note of that and polish it up before the final deployment." (Then write it down!).
- **Scenario B: The Client asks for a NEW feature.**
  - _Client:_ "Can you also add a library management system?"
  - _Don't say:_ "Yes, I can do that easily." (Don't work for free!).
  - _Do say:_ "That‚Äôs a great idea. Let‚Äôs launch this version first, and we can discuss that as a 'Phase 2' update with a separate budget."

## üíº 4. Professionalism Tips (‡∑Ä‡∑ò‡∂≠‡∑ä‡∂≠‡∑ì‡∂∫‡∂∑‡∑è‡∑Ä‡∂∫)

- **Listen More, Talk Less:** Let the client play with the system. Watch where they click.
- **Confidence:** You built this. You know it better than they do. Stand tall.
- **Ownership:** If they ask a technical question you don't know, say: "I need to check the specific documentation for that library, I will get back to you by evening."
